1) How does the impact of a movie vary with respect to the time of the year it is released?¶

Movies come out at all times of the year. But we look forward to watching movies during the holidays or when we are free. So does that mean that there is a particular time when the movies make the maximum impact? And is it because the movies that come out during this time are particularly very good and attracts people? Let's find out.

In [99]:
#We first group the data by their release month and aggregate the mean.
data = movies_df.groupby('release_month').mean()
Our first observation.¶

For our first observation, plotting the variataion of the mean revenue of the different months were plotted against the respective months. The main objective is to determine if some particular months give higher yield compared to the rest.

In [126]:
plot_series_bar(data['revenue'], yaxis='revenue', xaxis='release month', title='Mean revenue for every month')
Holidays sell better¶

It is obvious from the given plot that the movies made more revenue during the months of May, June, July, November and December and the average revenue made by movies during the rest of the months are significantly lower. This makes sense because holidays are the best time to watch movies. Moreover, we see the variation of revenue close to the shape of a sine wave. This signifies that the average interest towards movies rises during the holidays and dips during the off holiday time.

How do other factors compare?¶

Comparing the mean revenue generated per month was one way to compare the variation of the movie impact according to the time of the year. Now, let us compare different other factors that determine the quality of a movie. For instance, let us look into other factors. Like, Do good quality movies( ones with higher vote average ) wait till it is the holiday season to generate better revenue? And so does that mean that it will play a major role in generating more revenue during the season? Let us find out. Also, vote count was also taken in account to compare it with the vote average because movies with higher vote average may not mean that the movie is of better quality. It could mean that only a few votes were recorded and thus cannot be efficiently judged.

In [101]:
normalised_df = pd.DataFrame(index=np.arange(1, 13, 1), columns=['revenue', 'vote_average', 'popularity'])

normalised_df['vote_count'] = (data['vote_count']-data['vote_count'].min())/(data['vote_count'].max()-data['vote_count'].min())
Plotting the data.¶

The data considered for this plot are, revenue, popularity and the average vote count. The data were normalised so that they could be compared easier.

In [125]:
plot_series_line(normalised_df, 'normalised values', 'month', 'comparing revenue, vote average and popularity')
Better impact than other factors.¶

When we compare the variation of factors like the revenue, vote average and populary, it is obvious that the popularity of the movie is inaccordance with the revenue generated by it. That is quite justifiable because popular movies sell better. But the striking result is that, the average popularity of movies is also high during the holiday season, this can be supported by the vote count recieved by the movies during this season. Another striking feature is that these factors(revenue and popularity) are more dependend on the time of the year than the vote average recieved by that movie. This signifies that more than the quality of a movie, the popularity is the major player in determining the success of a movie. And we know that the popularity is high during the mid year.

To conclude.¶

We can say that above the quality of a movie in general, a major key in determining the success of a movie is the time in which it is released. And this is because the general rise in popularity of movies during the holiday season. This is strongly supported by the fact that movies with higher vote average did not meet the expectations compared to the movies having lower vote averages during the holiday season. The limitation with this assessment is that many of the vote averages were recorded with insufficient number of voters, so that cannot be made an exact measure of comparing it to the other features. However from all the data that we have, it is clear that the movies do sell better during the holiday season.

We are very lucky to be alive during this time of the universe. We have withnessed the rise of movies from the very primitive animations to advanced CGI and image mapping technologies. Moreover, we are living in a world where everything is accessible to everyone world wide. This has inspired new ideas and experiments which gave us wonderful experiences. So, how was the growth of the movie industry throughout the ages? Let us find out.

3 Different trends.¶

We can observe here that these three parameters have changed over the years in three different styles. Let us analyse them.

1. Revenue¶

This is fairly obvious that with new movies being released all around the world, the movie industry definately makes a now compared to the previous years. Another major factor is the quantity vs quality of the movies in the dataset throughout the years. Older movies from other countries are not present inside the dataset, which in turn accounts for lower average revenue in the past.

2. Popularity¶

Talking about the popularity, this is proportional to the revenue but this has not increased compared to the revenue.

3. Vote average¶

This is the most striking feature of this data set. This shows the rapid decline of the average vote count of movies. The primary reason for this is because of the fact that, a lot of movies are being released now a days and most of the time the story does not make any sense. Another possibility to this is that, there is a lack of bad movies in the data set which was released before the 90s. Nevertheless, the vote average would have still been on the decline. The rise in popularity and the revenue with the declining vote average signifies over rated movies.

Conclusion¶

As we have analysed the data, we can see that the movies released during the holidays have better chances of success. Certainly this will change with the advent of Netflix and other streaming media. But the analysis of that is for another day. However one thing is very much clear, i.e a good movie does not always see success. The popularity it gains during the time which it gets released determines a lot.

There is an another aspect to this. With the modern ways of advertising, movies often tend to get overrated in the box office, this can lead to false ratings. And this can surprisingly works both ways, an average movie can be rated bad or good. We have seen that movies with moderate popularity before the 90s had considerably good vote average. But over popularity tends to decrease the rating for a movie. However, we can say that this gains revenue for them. The movie industry is in not facing any economic crisis, that's for sure and it won't anytime soon. Talking about the quality, except for some of the movies getting really bad ratings, most of the movies are of good quality.

The limitations¶

In this analysis, Some of the data were missing key points. This would have made a lot of differences in our analysis if it was present there. And also, the ratings of movies with very low popularity cannot be trusted as a few people's opinion does not create much impact. A major setback in my analysis however would be the fact that an entire year or an entire month of all the years were summarised. So there might have been months were there were good movies aside from the holiday season, but would've drowned under the huge aggregation. These are the limitations of my analysis.

So to answer it in a single line.

The movie industry is going in a very good direction, technology has made the possibilities endless and so will the filmakers continue to impress us.¶
